Homework 1: Lebesgue measurable sets and functions

  1. Vitali set $V$ is nonmeasurable. Choose one representative from each equivalence class of $[0,1]$ under $x \sim y \iff x-y\in\mathbb{Q}$, and prove the resulting set is not Lebesgue measurable.

  2. If $m$ is a countably additive, translation-invariant measure defined on a $\sigma$-algebra containing $V$, then $m(0,1)$ is either $0$ or $\infty$.

  3. Let $C\subset[0,1]$ be the Cantor set. Then $C$ is closed, hence $C\in\mathcal{B}$, and $m(C)=0$. Since Lebesgue measure is complete, every subset $N\subset C$ is Lebesgue measurable (and $m(N)=0$). Prove that not every subset of $C$ is Borel.

  4. Let $m$ denote Lebesgue measure on $\mathbb{R}$.

    (a) Prove that $m$ is translation invariant on Borel sets: $m(E+x)=m(E)$.

    (b) Use the completion of measures to show that translation invariance extends to all Lebesgue measurable sets.

  5. Lusin’s theorem on $\mathbb{R}$. Using only the interval version, prove that for measurable $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ and $\delta>0$, there exists a continuous $\varphi:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ such that $m(\{x: f(x)\neq\varphi(x)\})<\delta$.
